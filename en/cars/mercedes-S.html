Mercedes S-klasa AMG S400d

Carvia - Mercedes S-klasa AMG S400d

The Mercedes S-Class includes systems that can read road bumps and then adjust the suspension accordingly, as well as driver assistance technology that uses satellite navigation data to slow down and take curves smoothly. These are surprisingly clever things. Luxury limousines like the Mercedes S-Class are expected to carry passengers in the highest comfort, and Mercedes certainly seems to be offering the necessary ingredients. Seats offer all the possibilities of electric adjustment, and rider and passengers have a massage option to help crank up the luxury, regardless of the length of the journey. Despite the standard adjustable air suspension, the ride quality of the Mercedes S-Class is not entirely perfect in all situations. In the city, you can feel the effect of larger imperfections reflecting from inside to inside, although to a lesser extent than in the 7 series. However, as speed increases on the open road, driving becomes extremely flexible and comfortable. In this regard, the S-Class easily ranks with the best in the segment and is much better controlled than the Range Rover, which has a tendency to sink into corners and bounce more clearly on dips and crests. However, this is a Mercedes S-Class, but it doesn't stop there: depending on the model chosen, there are various options for further improvement. For example, Magic Body Control uses a stereo camera mounted on the windshield to scan the surface of the road in front of the vehicle, then pre-adjust the suspension to deal with it. It is amazingly effective as long as you barely notice their presence as you travel through a series of large bumps.
